from the desk of michael nutt,

Hi! My name is Michael Nutt and I build things. I'm also working on a few software projects. Occasionally I even write about what I do. This is my portfolio.

  • I built this mini-site for a friend who needed a portfolio. It provided a simple interface and an elegant display her resume and personal statements.

  • The aim of this application was to create an easy-to-use administration interface for a band's website.

  • The interface was meant to be straightforward for band members to easily add blog posts and show photos, but powerful enough for designers to tweak the design of the site.

  • Breakup At The Food Court was the name of a fake Ben Folds song: when he was asked by his label what songs he was working on, he made up some names since he wasn't working on any songs. The site tracks all of his shows, allowing fans to add setlists and upload photos.

  • BATFC was an interesting project to work on because it took a large amount of show data and presented it in a form that was quickly scannable.

  • An entry into a design contest; (I was really into Ben Folds music at the time) one of my other submissions won and was used for the site.

  • At SXSW 2006 I needed business cards but was not yet affiliated with a company. This shows the process I went through in designing them. The right-most card was the final result.

  • I consulted with my school's student government organization to create a new website to replace their old outdated one.

  • MetaRails is a project similar to Heroku to bring dynamic website development to the masses.

  • MetaRails was intented to also be useful to the advanced developer by giving a more spatial view of each project and showing how the project files related to one another.

  • Branding for a distributed hash table.

  • A new website conceptual mockup for the Church of the Nativity in Huntsville, AL.

  • Mockup of a personal site for a friend who wanted a place to display and sell her artwork.

  • A fake concert poster for the Rites of Spring concert festival. Designed in Photoshop and silkscreened in four layers.

  • The posters were distributed around campus in anticipation of the actual lineup announcement, which coincidentally included Ben Folds.

  • A study in digital photography manipulation.

  • A promotional poster for Vanderbilt University's humor publication

  • Project page for the Vanderbilt Computer Society's open source voting system, still in use.

  • "What to wear?", a study in daily clothing selection
